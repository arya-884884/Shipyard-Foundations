Pollution

Pollution is the introduction of harmful materials into the environment. These harmful materials are called pollutants. Pollutants can be natural, such as volcanic ash, or created by human activity, such as trash, factory waste etc. Pollution damages the quality of air, water and land posing serious risks to human health, wildlife, and entire ecosystems. Beyond the physical landscape, modern pollutants also include excessive light and noise that disrupt natural cycles.

Major Impacts of Pollution

  • Air: Respiratory diseases and global warming.
  • Water: Destruction of marine life and unsafe drinking water.
  • Soil: Loss of fertile land and effects natural food chain.
  • Light: Disturbs natural wildlife behavior and human sleep patterns.
  • Overall: Destroys nature and harms our health.
